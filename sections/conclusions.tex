
\section{Conclusion}

This research paper presented the design, implementation, and benchmarking of a hybrid quantum classical software stack that is addressing the present day middleware gap between distributed HPC resources and cloud based QPUs. The stack integrates MPI distributed classical computation, CUDA ready GPU acceleration, and access to the IBM Quantum QPU through a unified Python/C++ architecture. This middlware is validated through molecular ground state energy estimation by using the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE).

The results of this projects conducted experiments have demonstrated several key contributions:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{MPI distributed Pauli term evaluation produces measurable speedup.} The CPU-only distributed stack achieved a $1.18\times$ speedup on $H_{2}O$ (1086 Pauli terms) at $P=2$ MPI ranks compared to the serial baseline. The speedup scales directly with the number of distributable Pauli terms, confirming the middleware's partitioning strategy is effective for sufficiently complex molecules.

    \item \textbf{GPU acceleration resolves the CPU scaling bottleneck.} The GPU Aer backend achieved a $1.51\times$ speedup on $H_{2}O$ at $P=8$ over the serial baseline, compared to a $0.24\times$ regression on CPU-only at the same rank count. GPU strong scaling efficiency reached $71.1\%$ at $P=2$ and crucially did not degrade at $P \geq 4$, confirming Bayraktar et al.'s prediction that the $O(2^n)$ statevector construction was the primary bottleneck. The GPU weak scaling demonstrated a $6\times$ improvement at $P=8$ over the CPU path. These results were achieved on a consumer-grade NVIDIA GTX 1650 Mobile, representing a lower bound; data-center GPUs would yield proportionally greater speedups.

    \item \textbf{Near chemical accuracy is achievable with HWE ansatzes and through careful initialization.} The molecule $H_{2}$ achieved $2.4$ mHa error (which is approaching the $1.6$ mHa chemical accuracy threshold), and $H_{2}$ at $P=4$ achieved  $0.1$ mHa (within chemical accuracy). The strategy of near zero parameter initialization proved effective as it keeps the HWE ansatz within the physical particle number sector during the stage of early optimization. This delays the occurence of the variational principle violation, which is inherent to non-particle-conserving ansatzes such as HWE.

    \item \textbf{The communication masking architecture is validated.} The established masking metric $M = T_{\text{accel}} / T_{\text{comm}}$ remained well above unity (by 2--3 orders of magnitude) in the simulator path ($M = 12$ -- $9{,}000$). This confirms the classical computation fully masks MPI communication overhead, which validates the central architecture hypothesis that predicted distributing the VQE's computationally heavy classical subroutines across MPI ranks would not introduce a communication bottleneck but instead a computational speedup.

    \item \textbf{End-to-end IBM QPU integration is functional.} The stack successfully completed 10 VQE iterations on IBM's \texttt{ibm\_marrakesh} processor, where each iteration averaged $42.1$s of wall-clock time (time dominated by the QPU round-trip time latency). While the $0.97$ Ha error in QPU accuracy reflects the NISQ limitations of 10 iterations with shot noise and minimal error mitigation, the results still validates the complete middleware pipeline: problem definition, Hamiltonian construction, ansatz transpilation, PUB batching, cloud QPU submission, results aggregation, and the SPSA parameter update.

    \item \textbf{Checkpoint resilience and stress testing confirm operational robustness.} The stack proves to withstand the simulated QPU latency spikes (introduced to 0.5--2.0s random delays across 10 iterations), recovers from mid-run checkpoint deletion tests without showing data loss, and maintains MPI synchronization throughout all failure scenarios introduced.
\end{enumerate}

The primary limitation of this stack remains the $O(2^n)$ statevector construction cost, where each MPI rank performs it independently. While GPU acceleration reduced this bottleneck sufficiently for scaling to continue at $P \geq 4$, the consumer-grade GTX 1650 Mobile's limited memory bandwidth ($\sim$128 GB/s) prevented cuStateVec from outperforming the CUDA thrust backend — a result attributable to hardware constraints rather than architectural limitations. Additionally, the HWE ansatz's particle-number violation, while mitigated through near-zero initialization and best-physical-energy tracking, represents a fundamental limitation that can only be fully resolved through particle-number-conserving ansatzes such as UCCSD.

This work demonstrates that a middleware-first approach to hybrid quantum-classical computing, focusing on orchestration, distribution, and latency masking instead of solely circuit level optimization, produces measurable performance improvements along with provideing a stable foundation for scaling to larger, more complex molecular systems as quantum hardware evolves.
