All tested molecules achieved $M \gg 1$, confirming that both the statevector construction and Pauli term evaluation (the acceleration workload) completely mask the MPI communication overhead. This result validates this projects core architectural claim that distributing a classical subroutine across MPI ranks will not introduce a communication bottleneck, so the Quantum 'Communication Wall' (that was identified by Weder et al.~\cite{weder2021quantum}) is not the main limiting factor when compute dominates.

Table~\ref{table:scaling_molecules} shows that all four molecules benefit from $P=2$, with the speedup proportional to the number of distributable Pauli terms: $H_{2}O$ (1086 terms) sees the largest improvement while $H_{2}$ (15 terms) sees negligible benefit. Table~\ref{table:scaling_efficiency} quantifies this for $H_{2}O$, where $P = 2$ achieved a $1.23\times$ speedup with $61.7\%$ efficiency. This demonstrates a benefit from utilizing the MPI distribution in this hybrid stack. The diminishing returns at $P=4$ and the regression at $P= 8$ are due to two main factors:   
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Statevector redundancy}: Each rank constructs a full $2^{n}$-dimensional statevector (the $O(2^n)$ cost) independently and without parallelization. Only Pauli term evaluations are distributed.
    \item \textbf{Shared memory contention}: All MPI ranks execute on a single physical host that shares CPU cache, memory bandwidth, and the OS scheduling. This bottleneck would be reduced by the usage of a multi-node cluster with a InfiniBand interconnect.          
\end{enumerate}

The scaling behavior is expected for significant improvements when handling larger molecules, ones that have more Pauli terms to be distributed ($H_{2}O$ = 1086 terms vs. $LiH$ = 631 terms). Along with the utilization of GPU accelerated statevector construction with cuQuantum, this would transfer the computational bottleneck from the per-rank statevector builds to a parallelizable Pauli term evaluation.

The total wall-clock time grows with problem size, which is expected because weak scaling simultaneously increases both the Hamiltonian complexity and the qubit count (4$\rightarrow$12$\rightarrow$14 qubits), meaning that the $O(2^n)$ statevector construction cost dominates. The MPI communication overhead grows sub-linearly: $T_{\text{comm}}$ per iteration increases from $25\;\mu$s ($P=1$) to $256$\,ms ($P=8$), but the per-term communication cost actually decreases from $\sim$17\,$\mu$s/term to $\sim$2.4\,$\mu$s/term, confirming that \texttt{MPI\_Allreduce} scales efficiently.

The masking metric $M$ drops from $\sim$1{,}400 at $P=2$ to $\sim$4.5 at $P \geq 4$, reflecting the shared-memory contention effect observed in the strong scaling results: 4--8 MPI ranks competing for 4 physical CPU cores inflates $T_{\text{comm}}$ relative to $T_{\text{accel}}$. On dedicated multi-node hardware with per-rank CPU resources, $M$ would remain higher. Importantly, $M > 1$ at all rank counts, confirming that even under the worst-case shared-memory conditions, classical computation still exceeds communication overhead.


\subsection{IBM Quantum QPU Results}
Table~\ref{table:ibm_results} presents the summary of results from the IBM QPU run, while Table~\ref{table:ibm_iterations} provides the per-iteration information. Over 10 iterations, the best energy improved from $-0.111\;E_h$ to $-0.171\;E_h$, representing a consistent downward trend that confirms the SPSA optimizer is functional on real quantum hardware. However, the final energy of $-0.171\;E_h$ remains far from the FCI reference of $-1.137\;E_h$ (an error of $0.966\;E_h$). This is expected for several reasons: 10 iterations is not enough for SPSA convergence (the simulator path required 200 iterations for $H_{2}$), 4096 shot sampling introduces significant variance that does not exist in a noiseless statevector path, and T-REx (resilience level 1) only handles measurement readout errors without addressing gate errors or decoherence on the physical qubits in the IBM runs. The non-monotonic energy trajectory, visible in the results of Table~\ref{table:ibm_iterations} (where the energy increases at iterations 3, 4, and 8) is expected of SPSA operating under shot noise, as each energy estimate has a statistical uncertainty of order $1/\sqrt{4096} \approx 0.016$.    

Each iteration averaged at $42.1$s of wall-clock time, with the metric dominated by the QPU RTT (31.9-60.4 s). This confirms that the cloud latency overhead that was predicted in this projects methodology, with the QPU RTT exceeding the classical computation time by orders of magnitude when compared to the simulator path/ Yet, the stack's architecture handled this without causing failure across all 10 iterations.

\subsubsection{cuStateVec Backend Comparison}
The cuStateVec backend, where the full statevector resides in GPU memory, was consistently slower than the GPU Aer backend on the test hardware (NVIDIA GTX 1650 Mobile, 4 GB GDDR6). For $H_{2}O$ at $P=2$, cuStateVec took $183.49$\,s compared to GPU Aer's $182.85$\,s, with the gap widening at $P=4$ ($242.11$\,s vs. $165.76$\,s). This result is explained by the hardware mismatch: Bayraktar et al.~\cite{bayraktar2023cuquantum} established that statevector simulation is memory-bandwidth-bound, and the GTX 1650's $\sim$128 GB/s GDDR6 bandwidth is over $15\times$ lower than the A100's 2,039 GB/s HBM2e. The overhead of GPU kernel launches and data transfers on this consumer hardware exceeds the benefit for circuits of 14 qubits or fewer. On data-center GPUs with HBM, cuStateVec would be expected to outperform the CPU Aer path significantly.

\subsection{Checkpoint Resilience}
The 6 layer diagnostic test has verified the checkpoint resilience across all runs. After 5 iterations run on $H_{2}$, the optimizer state ($\theta \in \mathbb{R}^{24}$) was then saved to \texttt{checkpoint\_iter\_0005.npy}. Upon the stack's restart, the VQE correctly resumed from iteration 6, using the saved $\theta$ and the energy trajectory continued on without discontinuity. This stack's checkpoint system rotates the saved files to keep only the 5 most recent saves (preventing unbounded disk usage during any long optimization runs).
