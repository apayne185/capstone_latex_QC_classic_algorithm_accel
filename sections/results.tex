In the stack, $H_{2}$ achieved an absolute error of 2.4 mHa, which is approaching the chemical accuracy threshold of 1.6 mHa (1 kcal/mol). A noteable remakr is that the serial baseline achieved a lower error on $LiH$ (2.2 mHa compared to 336.2 mHa) and with the error of $BeH_2$. This indicates the MPI distribution does not necessarily improve accuracy, as both versions of the stack use idential near zero initialization and the same SPSA hyperparameters, so the accuracy differences stem from the stochastic divergence in SPSA tragectories in difference execution tasks (the distributed \texttt{Allreduce}  vs serial summation) instead of from an algorithmic advantage. The molecule $BeH_{2}$ reached 39.4 mHa within this deterministic run, although in a separate stochastic trial (using a different seed) achieved 0.8 mHa (within chemical accuracy). This demonstrate that the stack is capable of reaching the target when the SPSA trajectory is favorable.
The wall-clock comparison (Table~\ref{table:speedup}) shows that the distributed stack achieves meaningful speedup only on larger molecules: $1.11\times$ on $BeH_{2}$ (666 Pauli terms) and $1.18\times$ on $H_{2}O$ (1086 terms), where the parallelizable Pauli term evaluation outweighs the MPI coordination overhead. For smaller molecules ($H_{2}$, $LiH$), the overhead of MPI initialization and \texttt{Allreduce} communication exceeds the benefit of distributing fewer Pauli terms.

\subsection{Masking Metric}
The masking metric $M = T_{\text{accel}} / T_{\text{comm}}$  was measured once each iteration for all molecules. The values are shown in Table~\ref{table:masking}.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.4}
\caption{Communication masking metric $M = T_{\text{accel}} / T_{\text{comm}}$ across execution paths. $M \geq 1$ indicates classical computation fully masks communication latency. In the simulator path, $T_{\text{comm}}$ is the MPI \texttt{Allreduce} time; in the IBM path, $T_{\text{comm}}$ is the QPU round-trip time.}
\label{table:masking}
\begin{tabular}{|l|c|l|}
\hline
\textbf{Execution Path} & \textbf{$M$ Range} & \textbf{Interpretation} \\ \hline
Simulator $P$=1 & $2{,}200$--$9{,}000$  & Compute dominates by $\sim 3$ orders of magnitude  \\ \hline
Simulator $P$=2 & $12$--$5{,}400$  & Compute dominates; higher variance from MPI timing  \\ \hline
IBM QPU & $32$--$60$ & QPU round-trip ($\sim$32--60\,s) still masked by classical setup \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

All tested molecules achieved $M \gg 1$, confirming that both the statevector construction and Pauli term evaluation (the acceleration workload) completely mask the MPI communication overhead. This result validates this projects core architectural claim that distributing a classical subroutine across MPI ranks will not introduce a communication bottleneck, so the Quantum 'Communication Wall' (that was identified by Weder et al.~\cite{weder2021quantum}) is not the limiting factor when compute dominates.

\label{table:scaling_efficiency}
\begin{tabular}{|c|r|c|c|c|}
\hline
\textbf{Ranks ($P$)} & \textbf{$T_{\text{total}}$ (s)} & \textbf{Speedup ($T_1/T_P$)} & \textbf{Ideal Speedup} & \textbf{Efficiency} \\ \hline
1 & $210.42$ & $1.00\times$ & $1.00\times$ & $100\%$  \\ \hline
2 & $170.66$ & $1.23\times$ & $2.00\times$ & $61.7\%$ \\ \hline
4 & $305.87$ & $0.69\times$ & $4.00\times$ & $17.2\%$ \\ \hline
8 & $1038.50$ & $0.20\times$ & $8.00\times$ & $2.5\%$  \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Here, $P = 2$ achieved a $1.23\times$ speedup with $61.7\%$ efficiency. This demonstrates a proven benefit from utilizing the MPI distribution in this hyrbid stack. Here, the diminishing returns at $P=4$ and the regression at $P= 8$ are due to two main factors:   
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Statevector redundancy}: Each rank independently constructs a full $2^{n}$-dimensional statevector (the $O(2^n)$ cost), which is not parallelized. In this stack, only Pauli term evaluations are distributed.
    \item \textbf{Shared memory contention}: All MPI ranks execute on a single physical host that shares CPU cache, memory bandwidth, and the OS scheduling. The usage of a multi-node cluster with a InfiniBand interconnect would reduce this detected bottleneck.         
\end{enumerate}

The scaling behavior is expected for significant imrpovements when handling larger molecules that have more Pauli terms to be distributed ($H_{2}O$ = 1086 terms vs. $LiH$ =631 terms), and when utilizing GPU accelerated statevector construction with cuQuantum. This would transfer the computational bottleneck from the per rank statevector builds to a parallelizable Pauli term evaluation.

\subsection{Checkpoint Resilience}
The 6 layer diagnostic test has verified the checkpoint resilience across all runs. After 5 iterations run on $H_{2}$, the optimizer state ($\theta \in \mathbb{R}^{24}$) was then saved to \texttt{checkpoint\_iter\_0005.npy}. Upon the stack's restart, the VQE correctly resumed from iteration 6, using the saved $\theta$ and the energy trajectory continued on without discontinuity. This stack's checkpoint system rotates the saved files to keep only the 5 most recent saves (preventing unbounded disk usage during any long optimization runs).
