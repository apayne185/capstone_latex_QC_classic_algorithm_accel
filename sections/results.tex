$H_{2}$ achieved an absolute error of 2.4 mHa, which is approaching the chemical accuracy threshold of 1.6 mHa (1 kcal/mol). The molecule $BeH_{2}$ reached 39.4 mHa within this deterministic run, although in a separate stochastic trial (using a different seed) achieved 0.8 mHa (within chemical accuracy). This demonstrate that the stack is capable of reaching the target when the SPSA trajectory is favorable.  
Here, the serial baseline produced a significantly worse set of results on every inputted molecule, which is due to it initializing parameters uniformly across $[0, 2\pi]$. This action immediately places the HWE ansatz in unphysical Hilbert space sectors. The distributed stack's near zero initialization instead keeps the initial state closer to Hartree-Fock reference, which dramatically improves the convergence behavior.


\subsection{Masking Metric}
The masking metric $M = T_{\text{accel}} / T_{\text{comm}}$  was measured once each iteration for all molecules. The values are shown in Table~\ref{table:masking}.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.4}
\begin{tabular}{|l|c|c|c|}
\hline
\textbf{Molecule} & \textbf{Median $M$} & \textbf{Range} & \textbf{$M \geq 1$?} \\ \hline
H\textsubscript{2} & $\sim 50$    & 2--1700    & Yes \\ \hline
LiH & $\sim 1800$  & 30--4500   & Yes \\ \hline
BeH\textsubscript{2} & $\sim 160$   & 12--5200   & Yes \\ \hline
H\textsubscript{2}O & $\sim 90$    & 12--5100   & Yes \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\caption{Masking metric ($M = T_{\text{accel}} / T_{\text{comm}}$) across the molecules at $P=2$. $M \gg 1$ indicates that classical computation dominates MPI communication overhead.}
\label{table:masking}
\end{table}

All tested molecules achieved $M \gg 1$, confirming both the statevector construction and Pauli term evaluation (the acceleration workload) completely mask the MPI communication overhead. This result validates this projects core architectural claim of distributing a classical subroutine across MPI ranks will not introduce a communication bottleneck, so the 'Communication Wall' (that was identified by Weder et al.~\cite{weder2021quantum}) is not the limiting factor when compute dominates.


\subsection{Strong Scaling}
\label{sec:results_scaling}

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.4}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|}
\hline
\textbf{Ranks ($P$)} & \textbf{$T_{\text{total}}$ (s)} & \textbf{Speedup ($S$)} & \textbf{Efficiency ($E$)} \\ \hline
1 & 0.962 & 1.00$\times$ & 100\% \\ \hline      
2 & 0.682 & 1.41$\times$ & 71\% \\ \hline   
4 & 0.644 & 1.49$\times$ & 37\% \\ \hline
8 & 1.755 & 0.55$\times$ & 7\% \\ \hline     
\end{tabular}     
\caption{Strong scaling results for $LiH$ (12 qubits, 631 Pauli terms, 10 iterations).  All ranks were executed in a Docker container on a single host.}
\label{table:scaling}
\end{table}

$P = 2$ achieved a $1.41\times$ speedup at $71\%$ efficiency, which demonstrates a proven benefit from MPI distribution being utilized within this stack. Here, the diminishing returns at $P= 4$ and the regression at $P= 8$ are due to two factors:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Statevector redundancy}: Each rank will independently construct a full $2^{12}$- dimensional statevector (the $O(2^n)$ cost), which is not parallelized. In this stack, only Pauli term evaluation is distributed.
    \item \textbf{Shared-memory contention}: All ranks execute on a single physical host, therefore sharing the CPU cache, memory bandwidth, and the OS scheduling. The use of a multi-node cluster with a InfiniBand interconnect would reduce this bottleneck.
\end{enumerate}

The scaling behavior is expected for significant imrpovements when handling larger molecules that have more Pauli terms to be distributed ($H_{2}O$ = 1086 terms vs. $LiH$ =631 terms), and when utilizing GPU accelerated statevector construction with cuQuantum. This would transfer the computational bottleneck from the per rank statevector builds to a parallelizable Pauli term evaluation.

\subsection{Checkpoint Resilience}
The 6 layer diagnostic test has verified the checkpoint resilience across all runs. After 5 iterations run on $H_{2}$, the optimizer state ($\theta \in \mathbb{R}^{24}$) was then saved to \texttt{checkpoint\_iter\_0005.npy}. Upon the stack's restart, the VQE correctly resumed from iteration 6, using the saved $\theta$ and the energy trajectory continued on without discontinuity. This stack's checkpoint system rotates the saved files to keep only the 5 most recent saves (preventing unbounded disk usage during any long optimization runs).
